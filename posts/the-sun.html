The Sun

The Sun is the star at the center of our solar system. It is a nearly perfect ball of hot plasma, about 1.39 million km across — roughly 109 Earths wide. It holds about 99.86% of the solar system's mass and its gravity keeps all the planets in orbit. Its core reaches about 15 million °C, where hydrogen fuses into helium, releasing the light and heat that power life on Earth. The Sun is around 4.6 billion years old and sits about 150 million km (1 astronomical unit) from Earth.
